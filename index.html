The Beginning of my Engineering Career

In 2015 I landed my 1st job in an engineering environment, working as Engineering Support in a large manufacturing plant. My duties included: interpreting engineering drawings to determine work instructions for manufactoring plant labor to build the specified products. My duties expaned to include creating standardized instructions and procedures for machine operators and material handlers. I considered safety, efficiency, and lean manufacturing protocols (minimizing waste, maximizing profit).

2015

CAD Department

In 2016 I accepted a position as a CAD Technician at a worldwide pharmaceutical plant. I was responsible for maintaining and revising all of the facilities technical drawings, keeping them current to reflect "as-built conditions". This was a very unique work environment. I worked with engineers and drawings of every discipline. The plant I worked at was new, I got to Experience many milestones the company reached as it prepared for production. I made major contributions to the development of the location's CAD department organization and procedures. Our department was half maintenance, half project-oriented. I took great pride in my department and enjoyed the work I did, however I knew that I had more passions to explore and more achievements to conquer...

2016

Coding Boot Camp

I've been interested in computer programming for years, I've even tried some free tutorials online. After hitting many stumbling blocks, I realized that I needed a more intimate learning environment with human guidance and interaction. In the spring of 2018 I researched my options for formal training, I found Georgia Tech's Coding Boot Camp to be the best option available to me. I qualified for the program, prepared to dedicate my life to this program, and on September 17th 2018, I began Georgia Tech's Full-Time Coding Boot Camp, on-site in Midtown Atlanta, GA!
